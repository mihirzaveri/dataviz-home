Dataviz: Fall 2013

Here's some work that I did in the UC Berkeley Journalism School's data visualization class in fall 2013, taught by Shan Carter and Kevin Quealy of the NY Times.

  • A Bar Chart in D3: Recreating NY Times Piece on ESPN's Dominance

    Made a bar chart in R and then D3 recreating this NY Times bar chart on ESPN network's relative monthly price.

  • An Interactive Line Chart in D3: Recreating NY Times' Strikeouts Graphic

    Another recreation (rather attempted recreation) of a NY Times graphic, this time with interactivity (click on a team and the line will change).

  • Intro to Mapping With R

    A mapping exercise using a California county shapefile and joining it with state demographic data to show the percent Hispanic population per county. Also my first lesson in saving my R code, never to be forgotten.

  • A Breaking News Exercise: Origins of Chicago's Guns

    We did a "quick" (nowhere close to breaking news quick) exercise using a dataset that listed by county where guns recovered in Chicago came from. The link above is the result of the R code, and here is the code itself.

  • Final Project

    We spent some of the final weeks in class reporting and producing our own final project. I chose to focus mine on missed votes in Congress, particularly testing the assumption that legislators prioritize campaigning over making law. Lots of R and D3 went into this, link to R code.
